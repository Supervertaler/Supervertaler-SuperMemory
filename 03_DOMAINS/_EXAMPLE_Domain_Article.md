# Legal (EN-US → NL-BE)

## Domain overview
Legal translation between US English and Belgian Dutch covers contracts,
regulatory documents, compliance materials, corporate governance, and
litigation support. Belgian Dutch legal terminology often differs
significantly from Netherlands Dutch due to the distinct legal systems.

## Key conventions
- **Register:** Strictly formal. No contractions, no colloquialisms.
- **Passive voice:** Commonly used in legal Dutch. Do not convert to active voice even if the source does.
- **Latin terms:** Preserve Latin legal terms (e.g., "force majeure", "bona fide") unless the client style guide specifies a Dutch equivalent.
- **Shall/must distinction:** US legal "shall" → "zal" (obligation) or "dient te" (stronger obligation). "Must" → "moet". Maintain the distinction consistently.

## Common pitfalls
| Source term | Wrong translation | Correct translation | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| jurisdiction | jurisdictie | rechtsmacht (BE) | "Jurisdictie" is NL-NL; Belgian Dutch uses "rechtsmacht" |
| governing law | toepasselijk recht | toepasselijk recht | Often confused with "geldend recht" which has a subtly different meaning |
| liability | verantwoordelijkheid | aansprakelijkheid | "Verantwoordelijkheid" = responsibility; "aansprakelijkheid" = legal liability |
| indemnify | schadeloosstellen | vrijwaren | "Schadeloosstellen" is compensation; "vrijwaren" is the correct legal term for indemnification |
