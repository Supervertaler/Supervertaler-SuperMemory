# General Style Guide: EN-US → NL-BE

## Punctuation and formatting
- **Quotation marks:** Use Dutch-style double quotes: "dubbele aanhalingstekens" (not guillemets unless client specifies)
- **Lists:** Follow source formatting. Do not add bullets or numbering not present in the source.
- **Capitalization:** Dutch uses lowercase for nationalities, languages, days, and months (unlike English). "Monday" → "maandag", "Dutch" → "Nederlands".

## Numbers and dates
- **Decimal separator:** comma (3,14)
- **Thousands separator:** period (1.000.000)
- **Currency:** EUR symbol before number, no space: €1.500,00
- **Dates:** DD/MM/YYYY or written: "15 januari 2026"
- **Time:** 24-hour format: 14:30 (not 2:30 PM)
- **Percentages:** 15% (no space before %)

## Address and register
- **Formal (default):** "u" / "uw"
- **Informal (if client requests):** "je" / "jouw"
- **Gender-neutral:** Use "zij/hun" or rephrase to avoid gendered pronouns when the source is gender-neutral.

## Common decisions
| Decision | Rule | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Anglicisms | Avoid where a good Dutch alternative exists | Client preference unless domain-specific loanword |
| Compound words | Follow Dutch spelling rules (aaneenschrijven) | "e-mailbericht" not "e-mail bericht" |
| Abbreviations | Expand on first use, then abbreviate | "gebruikersinterface (UI)" |
| Passive voice | Preserve if in source; do not convert | Maintains register and structure |

## Client overrides
Individual client profiles in [[01_CLIENTS]] may override these defaults.
Always check the client profile before applying general rules.
